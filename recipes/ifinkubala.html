IFINKUBALA

Description

Ifinkubala are dried caterpillars, a popular and nutritious Zambian delicacy (also known as Mopane worms) rich in protein, essential amino acids, iron, and potassium, often enjoyed as a snack or served with nshima (porridge) after being rehydrated, fried with onions and tomatoes, and seasoned. They are hand-picked during the rainy season, cleaned, dried, and then prepared by soaking and frying until they reach a desired crunchy or soft texture.

INGREDIENTS

  • Dried Ifinkubala: Typically 1 to 3 cups.
  • Cooking Oil: Approximately 200ml or 3 to 4 tablespoons for frying.
  • Tomatoes: 1 to 2 medium-sized tomatoes, diced or grated.
  • Onion: 1 medium-sized onion, chopped.
  • Salt: To taste.

OPTIONAL ENHANCEMENTS

  • Vegetables: Green or red bell peppers and okra.
  • Spices & Flavorings: Garlic, chili peppers, black pepper, or curry powder.
  • Sauces: A small amount of peri-peri sauce or tomato paste.
STEPS
  1. Soak: Wash dried ifinkubala in cold water, then soak in warm water until soft (can take several hours or overnight).
  2. Drain: Drain the water thoroughly.
  3. Fry: Heat oil in a pan and fry the caterpillars for about 20 minutes.
  4. Add aromatics: Add grated tomatoes and chopped onions, then cover and cook for another 10 minutes.
  5. Season: Add salt to taste and cook for a final 10 minutes.
  6. Serve: Enjoy hot!
