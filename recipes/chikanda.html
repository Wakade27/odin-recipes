Chikanda

Description

Chikanda is a popular, vegan Zambian delicacy made from ground wild orchid tubers, pounded peanuts, and spices, known for its unique texture resembling polony or sausage, served as a snack, side dish with nshima, or celebratory food. Its preparation involves boiling the mixture and often baking it on top for a browned crust, offering plant-based protein and energy.

Ingredients

  • Chikanda (Orchid Tuber) Powder: Approximately 1 cup (250g)
  • Pounded Groundnuts (Peanuts): 2 to 3.5 cups (500g).
  • Baking Soda: 1 to 2 tablespoons (crucial for browning and thickening).
  • Water: 3 to 4.5 cups (or up to 1 liter).
  • Seasonings: Salt to taste and chili (optional).

Steps

  1. Prepare the Base: Mix the pounded groundnuts with water in a medium pot to form a light paste or slurry.
  2. Initial Cooking: Place the pot on a charcoal brazier (preferred for traditional flavor) and stir continuously until the groundnut mixture is well-cooked, which takes about 10â€“15 minutes.
  3. Incorporate Chikanda: Gradually sprinkle the chikanda powder into the boiling groundnut paste. Stir vigorously and continuously with a wooden cooking stick to prevent lumps.
  4. Add Thickening Agents: Dissolve the baking soda in a small amount of water and add it to the mixture along with salt and chili. The mixture will begin to thicken and darken as you stir.
  5. Cook to Thickness: Continue stirring vigorously until the mixture is very thick and starts coming away from the sides of the pot or the cooking stick.
  6. Baking Process: Smooth the top of the mixture. Cover the pot with a lid and place hot charcoal directly on top of the lid (turning it upside down if necessary) to create a "top-down oven" effect. Bake for roughly 30 minutes on low heat until the top is golden brown.
  7. Cool and Slice: Remove the pot from the heat and allow the chikanda to cool completely. Once firm, turn the pot upside down to release the "loaf" onto a plate and slice it into pieces for serving.
  8. Enjoy!
